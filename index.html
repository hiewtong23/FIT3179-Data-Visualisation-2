Mapping Malaysia’s 4G Connectivity — 2022

This dashboard visualises Malaysia’s 4G availability, download performance, and socioeconomic indicators across states using multiple data visualisation idioms.

4G Network Availability by State (Q1 2022)

The availability of 4G networks varies notably across the country, with a clear difference between Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia.

Almost every state in Peninsular Malaysia records availability above 90%. In contrast, Sabah and Sarawak show comparatively lower availability, which indicates challenges in expanding reliable network access across more remote regions.

Median Download Speeds Across Malaysian States

Central and western parts of Peninsular Malaysia show higher download speeds.

East Malaysian states such as Sabah and Sarawak record slower median speeds, again highlighting the digital divide between urban and rural regions.

Year-on-Year Improvement in 4G Availability (2021–2022)

Each line connects 4G availability between Q1 2021 (grey) and Q1 2022 (blue), showing improvement by state.

4G availability improved across all Malaysian states between Q1 2021 and Q1 2022.

The largest gains occurred in Kelantan (+12.4%), Pahang (+11.1%), and Terengganu (+11.0%), showing strong progress in the East Coast.

Urban areas like Kuala Lumpur and Pulau Pinang saw smaller increases due to already high coverage levels. Overall, the gap between states is narrowing, reflecting more balanced nationwide connectivity.

Do States with Higher 4G Availability Also Enjoy Faster Speeds?

Interestingly, high availability does not always translate to higher speeds.

For instance, states like Perlis and Kelantan have relatively strong 4G coverage yet show noticeably lower download speeds — possibly due to network congestion or bandwidth limitations.

Meanwhile, areas such as Putrajaya, Pahang, and Johor combine strong coverage with high speeds, indicating efficient and stable network performance.

Does higher income tend to have better 4G coverage?

Contrary to expectation, the chart shows a slightly negative correlation — states with higher 4G availability tend to have lower mean household income.

For instance, Putrajaya and several urban states exhibit very high 4G availability but comparatively lower income levels, while Sarawak and Perak show higher incomes despite lower coverage.

This pattern suggests that while connectivity is widespread, economic prosperity does not directly correspond to mobile network reach.

Does Inequality Affect 4G Improvement? (Malaysia, Q1 2021–Q1 2022)

The chart reveals a slightly negative relationship between income inequality and 4G improvement.

Most Peninsular Malaysia states cluster around moderate inequality (0.35–0.37) and steady improvement, while East Malaysian states (Sabah and Sarawak) exhibit higher inequality but smaller gains.

States with fairer income distribution tend to experience stronger network improvements, hinting that social and digital equity are interconnected.
